# What Happens at a Police Station Interview?

**Last updated:** 14 June 2026

## Quick answer

A police station interview under caution is a formal investigative step. Clients and instructing firms often need a plain explanation of the process before attendance — not case-specific advice.

## Who this is for

- criminal defence firms and clients needing police station representation in Kent and Medway

## What happens when someone is interviewed under caution

After arrest or voluntary attendance, the police may conduct a recorded interview under caution. The caution explains that answers may be used in evidence. The suspect has the right to free legal advice at the police station. A solicitor or accredited police station representative attends, reviews disclosure, takes instructions in private, and advises on whether to answer questions, put forward a prepared statement, or go no comment.

Practitioners should keep notes contemporaneous and seek supervising solicitor input where case complexity exceeds accreditation scope. ## Voluntary interview vs custody interview

Voluntary interviews may take place at a police station without arrest. The same caution and recording standards often apply. Clients sometimes assume voluntary means informal — firms should explain that legal representation remains important and that attendance is not necessarily "optional" in a practical sense.

## Role of the legal representative

The representative protects the client's rights, ensures PACE compliance, and provides a clear record for the instructing firm. They do not decide guilt or innocence. Advice is based on disclosure, instructions, and law — including adverse inference considerations where a no comment interview is proposed.

## After the interview

The client may be released under investigation (RUI), bailed with or without conditions, charged, or released without charge. The attendance note and any handover to the firm should set out next steps and bail dates clearly.

## FAQ

### Should everyone answer police questions?

No single approach suits every case. Advice depends on disclosure and instructions from a qualified lawyer.

### Is a voluntary interview less serious?

It can still be used in evidence. Legal advice before attendance is prudent.

### Who can attend with the client?

A solicitor or accredited police station representative; youths and vulnerable persons may require an appropriate adult.
