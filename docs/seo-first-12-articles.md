## 1. The First Hour After a Kent Arrest: What to Do

- **Site (owner):** policestationagent.com — owns *after-arrest* (CLEAR)
- **Slug:** `first-hour-after-arrest-kent`
- **Category:** Arrest & Custody · **Primary keyword:** what to do after arrest kent
- **Schema:** BlogPosting
- **Meta title:** Arrested in Kent? What to Do in the First Hour | PSA
- **Meta description:** Arrested in Kent? The first hour matters most. Your right to free legal advice, what to say, and how to get a representative to the custody suite fast.

#### Answer first

If you have been arrested in Kent, the single most important thing in the first hour is to ask for free, independent legal advice and to wait for that advice before being interviewed. Legal advice at the police station is free to everyone, regardless of income, and is available 24 hours a day.

You do not have to answer questions before you have spoken to a solicitor or accredited police station representative. Asking for legal advice does not make you look guilty and cannot be used against you.

#### What happens when you arrive in custody

On arrival you will see the custody officer, who must open a custody record, explain why you are being detained, and tell you your rights under PACE Code C: the right to free legal advice, the right to have someone told you have been arrested, and the right to consult the Codes of Practice.

You will be searched, your property logged, and your detention authorised. The custody clock generally allows the police to detain you for up to 24 hours before charge, extendable to 36 hours by a senior officer, and up to 96 hours only with magistrates' authority.

#### Say these three things

First: 'I want free legal advice.' Second: 'Please tell [name] where I am.' Third: nothing about the allegation until your adviser arrives.

If you are under 18 or a vulnerable adult, an appropriate adult must also be arranged before any interview — say so if it applies to you or your family member.

#### How to get a representative to a Kent custody suite quickly

When you ask for legal advice, the police contact the Defence Solicitor Call Centre, or your chosen firm directly if you name one. You can name Police Station Agent and we will arrange cover at Kent and Medway custody suites, including out of hours.

Time matters because disclosure, interview, and decisions to charge or release often happen quickly. Having a representative present means your account is recorded properly and your rights are protected from the start.

#### Answer first

Yes — you can get a solicitor or accredited representative at a Kent police station at any time of day or night. The right to free legal advice in custody applies 24 hours a day, including weekends and bank holidays.

The police cannot lawfully interview most detainees who have asked for legal advice until that advice has been provided, so asking protects you even at 3am.

#### Why nights and weekends are different

Arrests peak in the evenings and at weekends, and custody suites operate around the clock. Duty schemes and firms provide out-of-hours cover precisely because interviews and charging decisions do not wait for office hours.

If you name a firm, the custody officer contacts them; otherwise the Defence Solicitor Call Centre allocates a duty solicitor. Either way the advice is free to you.

#### How to request cover at a Kent or Medway suite

Tell the custody officer you want free legal advice and, if you wish, name Police Station Agent. We arrange representation across Kent and Medway custody suites, including overnight.

If you are calling on behalf of a family member, give the custody suite location and, if known, the custody reference. We can liaise with the custody officer to confirm attendance.

## 3. How Much Does a Police Station Solicitor Cost in Kent?

- **Site (owner):** policestationagent.com — owns *cost (conversion)* (CLEAR)
- **Slug:** `private-police-station-solicitor-cost-kent`
- **Category:** Your Rights · **Primary keyword:** police station solicitor cost
- **Schema:** BlogPosting
- **Meta title:** How Much Does a Police Station Solicitor Cost? (Kent)
- **Meta description:** Police station legal advice is free for everyone. When might you pay privately, what private cover includes, and how Kent fees typically work.

#### Answer first

Legal advice at the police station is free to everyone in England & Wales, whatever your income — this is not the same as legal aid for court, which can be means-tested. So in custody you do not have to pay to be advised and represented in interview.

Some people still choose private representation, for example to instruct a specific firm, for continuity into any later case, or for private (non-legal-aid) work. That is a choice, not a requirement.

#### Free vs private: what's the difference

Free police-station advice is funded by the Legal Aid Agency and delivered by duty solicitors or your chosen firm. It covers attendance, consultation, and interview representation.

Private representation is paid by you and may be charged at a fixed fee for attendance or an hourly rate. It can be useful where you want a particular solicitor, or where the matter sits outside legal aid scope.

#### How private Kent fees typically work

Private police-station fees vary by firm and by the time of day, the location of the custody suite, and how long attendance takes. Many firms quote a fixed attendance fee with any additional time charged separately; always ask for the basis of charge in writing before instructing.

Whichever route you choose, the value is the same: a trained representative who reviews disclosure, advises on whether to answer questions, and protects your position.

## 4. PSRAS Exam Format Explained (2026)

- **Site (owner):** psrtrain.com — owns *PSRAS exam* (CLEAR)
- **Slug:** `psras-exam-format-pass-mark-2026`
- **Category:** PSRAS Prep · **Primary keyword:** psras exam format
- **Schema:** BlogPosting
- **Meta title:** PSRAS Exam Format Explained (2026) | PSR Train
- **Meta description:** How the PSRAS is structured for police station representatives: the written exam, the Critical Incidents Test, the portfolio, and how to prepare.

#### Answer first

The Police Station Representatives Accreditation Scheme (PSRAS) is the route accredited representatives take to advise clients at the police station. It combines assessed knowledge, an assessed practical exercise (the Critical Incidents Test), and a portfolio of supervised police-station attendances.

Always confirm the current structure, components and pass standard with the SRA and the assessment organisation, because requirements are updated from time to time.

#### The written/knowledge assessment

The knowledge element tests core law and procedure: PACE and the Codes of Practice (especially Code C and Code D), the caution and the right to silence, detention and the custody clock, vulnerable suspects and appropriate adults, and professional conduct.

Most candidates prepare with timed multiple-choice practice so they can answer accurately under time pressure rather than only recognising the law when they see it.

#### The Critical Incidents Test (CIT)

The CIT is a practical assessment of how you handle a realistic police-station scenario: identifying the issues, prioritising client consultation, and making defensible decisions on advice. It rewards structured thinking, not memorised quotes.

Practising scenarios out loud — issue-spotting, then a clear decision trail — is the most effective preparation.

#### The portfolio

Alongside the assessments you build a portfolio evidencing real, supervised police-station attendances. This is firm-led: your supervising solicitor signs off competence against the standards.

Start collecting structured attendance evidence early; good contemporaneous notes make portfolio sign-off far easier.

## 5. How to Pass the Critical Incidents Test (CIT)

- **Site (owner):** psrtrain.com — owns *CIT* (CLEAR)
- **Slug:** `how-to-pass-critical-incidents-test`
- **Category:** CIT · **Primary keyword:** critical incidents test
- **Schema:** BlogPosting
- **Meta title:** How to Pass the Critical Incidents Test (CIT) | PSR Train
- **Meta description:** A structured method for the PSRAS Critical Incidents Test: spot the issues, prioritise consultation, and give a defensible decision trail.

#### Answer first

The CIT rewards a repeatable method, not recall. Read the scenario, identify the legal and welfare issues, prioritise private consultation with the client, then state a clear, justified decision on advice.

Examiners want to see safe, structured reasoning under pressure — the same thing a real custody case demands.

#### A four-step framework

1) Issues: list the Code C points (detention basis, custody clock, vulnerability/appropriate adult, fitness to interview). 2) Disclosure: what do you know, and what is missing? 3) Consultation: what advice follows from the disclosure and the client's instructions? 4) Decision: answer, no comment, or prepared statement — and why.

Saying the framework aloud keeps you calm and makes your reasoning visible to the assessor.

#### Common mistakes that cost marks

Jumping to 'no comment' without reasoning; ignoring welfare and appropriate-adult triggers; failing to note what disclosure is missing; and giving advice that does not match the client's instructions.

Practising several scenarios end-to-end fixes most of these before the assessment.
